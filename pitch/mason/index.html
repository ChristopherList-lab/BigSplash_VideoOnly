The Situation

A great story, almost no video telling it.

Mason has earned its spot on the Texas Monthly five most beautiful squares list. Sandstone storefronts, the Odeon Theater, the Llano River, eight wineries, topaz access on two ranches, a bat colony at Eckert James that runs May through October. The assets are rare.

The problem is that none of them appear on film on any Chamber-owned channel. masontx.org and destinationmason.com run on static photography and text. The Chamber Instagram sits at 737 followers despite 1,743 posts. No YouTube channel exists. No resident testimonial has been recorded. Someone searching "moving to Mason TX" lands on exploretexas.com, a third-party SEO page that loads with zero motion content.

Whoever shoots first owns the relocation surface for the next decade.

Why Now

Two windows open at the same time.

Window One

HB 3179 Just Unlocked County HOT Funds.

The 89th Texas Legislature authorized Mason County Commissioners Court to impose a county hotel occupancy tax in unincorporated areas. Revenue is statutorily restricted to tourism promotion.

Per Texas Tax Code, video advertising and promotional campaigns are an explicit eligible use. The Chamber has a funding path outside dues for the first time.

Window Two

The Founder of Big Splash Moves to Mason May 1.

Christopher List is relocating to Mason for 60 days with a full production kit. Cinema camera, drone, audio, lighting, edit suite.

Closing wildflower season on the way in. Bat emergence opens May 10. Roundup weekend lands in late June. Every shot on this page is reachable from the square in under 20 minutes.
