01 · Positioning

The woman this is for.

She's doing her best. She loves Jesus and says "babe" a lot. She's somewhere in the messy middle and she's wondering if she's the only one.

The promise

  • Awkward honesty — no performing, no polish
  • Jesus in the mess, not Jesus on a pedestal
  • Marriage, motherhood, mid-life — with the jokes left in
  • Permission to be real instead of perfect
  • Real talk, real laughs, real faith

What it's not

  • Not inspirational-quote wallpaper
  • Not a highlight reel or a "glow-up" brand
  • Not sermon-length, not theology-heavy
  • Not anti-wine, anti-humor, or anti-joy
  • Not a pick-me, fix-me, or fix-yourself brand
02 · Voice

The trinity.

Every caption, every carousel, every reel should land somewhere inside this triangle. If it reads like only one — push it toward the other two.

Grace

"I've also seen how He shows up in the mess — every single time."

Grit

"The kind of stuff that makes you question things, cry in the car, and wonder if God forgot your address."

Sarcasm

"Does anyone know which page of the Bible explains how to turn water into wine? Asking for a friend."

Signature lines · use verbatim
"Hey babe."Opener
"Jesus in the mess."Bio anchor
"Prayer is included in my business plan."Template quote
"Filled cups fill cups."Template quote
"If you know him, you know him. If you don't, you'd love him."The Shawn bit
"Women don't need more perfection — they need more permission."Positioning

Do

  • Open with "babe" or a direct question
  • Use sarcasm to disarm a heavy thought
  • Name the mess before you name the lesson
  • Drop the parenthetical aside ("which I should have done in 2024")
  • Write like you're texting one friend, not addressing a room

Don't

  • No "Here's the thing" or "Let that sink in"
  • No coach-speak: "lean into," "navigate," "level up"
  • No em dashes — use a period or parens
  • No three-part lists — two is sharper
  • No "the truth is" — just say the truth
